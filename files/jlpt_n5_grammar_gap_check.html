Verdict

Your N5 grammar foundation is strong — likely 80%+ ready. Marugoto A2+ has taken you well past most N5 patterns and into N4 territory. Compound verbs, ながら, ので, the consequence pattern with と, ~たり~たりする, the volitional, the prohibition pattern, the nominalizer の and んです — all confirmed in your sessions. These are mostly above N5 level.

The real gaps are surprisingly few: a handful of basic counters, the ~たい form for desires, and some particle subtleties. The "review" items are mostly things you've used implicitly but may not have drilled in JLPT format.

For N5, your work is mostly kanji + test format practice, not grammar acquisition. Save your serious grammar effort for the N4 push next year.

What this means for your prep

  1. Don't spend time learning new N5 grammar. You have it. Spend that time on kanji and test format.
  2. Drill counters and irregular number readings explicitly — these are guaranteed test items and easy to lose points on if you haven't memorized them cold.
  3. Take one mock test in early autumn 2026 to confirm format familiarity. The patterns in the "Review" column will reveal themselves as solid or shaky once you see test-format multiple choice.
  4. Use the gap items as a checklist — for each, write 3 example sentences in your notebook. If they come naturally, they're solid; if you stumble, they're real gaps.
  5. Save your serious grammar effort for N4 prep next year. The patterns above marked [N4] are the bridge — you've already started crossing it.
