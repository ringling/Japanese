Today's session

Saturday is the integration day. The week's pieces — kanji, vocabulary, counters, irregular readings — get pulled together into one cumulative test. The point isn't to relearn anything. It's to confirm what's solid and surface what isn't, before the next 7-day cycle begins.

Two activities, both in active-recall format:

Part 1 — Random kanji recall. All 13 kanji, in a different random order from Friday. Tests whether yesterday's recall was real or just short-term recency.
Part 2 — Read 10 prices aloud. Combines kanji recognition + counter readings + irregular hundred/thousand-readings into the kind of integrated task you'll face on the test (and in any real Japanese context).

Then a brief reflection — what's worth carrying into Week 2.

This is the integrated task — combining kanji recognition, counter readings, and the irregular hundred/thousand-readings that you've drilled all week. Treat it as a real-world simulation: you're at a Japanese store, looking at price tags, and you need to read them aloud confidently. The goal is fluency, not perfection.

PART 03

Reflection — what to carry forward

~5 min

The most useful thing you can do at the end of any study week is briefly notice what worked, what didn't, and what to adjust. Not in writing — just mentally, for 5 minutes, while you finish your tea or whatever.

Three questions

1. Which kanji are still shaky?

If any of the 13 kanji didn't come automatically in today's recall test, those are your "carry forward" items. They'll get spaced repetition in Week 2 just by virtue of being part of your existing kanji pool, but it helps to consciously know which they are.

2. Were the irregular readings sticky?

The big test items from this week were the irregular hundreds (さんびゃく, ろっぴゃく, はっぴゃく) and the counter sound changes (いっぽん, さんぼん). If those didn't come fluently in today's price-reading exercise, mark them for revisit during Week 5's mock test warm-up.

3. Did the Mon/Wed/Fri rhythm feel right?

Week 1 was the easiest week of the 8-week kanji intake (numbers, mostly familiar). If 30-40 minutes per session felt too light, you have headroom for harder weeks. If it felt heavy, the rhythm is correctly calibrated and Weeks 2-8 will feel similar — that's by design.

What you've actually accomplished this week

  • Met all 13 numbers kanji (一 二 三 四 五 六 七 八 九 十 百 千 万)
  • Drilled 7 counter types (〜つ, 〜本, 〜匹, 〜枚, 〜回, 〜階, 〜人)
  • Locked in the irregular hundreds (300, 600, 800)
  • Added ~14 numbers/money/age vocabulary words
  • Practiced handwriting on Wednesday
  • Three rounds of self-quiz (Wed kanji-recall + Fri 3 passes + today)

That's a full N5 kanji week's worth of work, in ~3 hours of focused study time. The remaining 7 weeks of the intake follow the same shape — by Week 8, you'll have all 104 kanji, all major counters, and the bulk of N5 vocabulary covered.
