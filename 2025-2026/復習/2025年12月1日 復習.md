## 文法 (Grammar Points)

### 1. ～という (called/named)
**Usage**: Used to explain names or introduce unfamiliar terms
- いちろうは いちばんめの おとこのこ という いみです = Ichirō means "first son"
- さいたまという ところ = a place called Saitama
- むらかみはるき という さっか = an author called Haruki Murakami

### 2. Counters for people
- ～にん (counter for people)
- なんにん = how many people
- いちにん、ににん、さんにん = one person, two people, three people
- さんにんきょうだい = three siblings

### 3. Family relationship expressions
- ～が います = to have (family members)
- いもうとが ふたり います = I have two younger sisters
- うえの いもうと = older younger sister
- したの いもうと = younger younger sister

### 4. Location and residence patterns
- ～に すんでいます = lives in/at...
- ～の ちかく = near...
- わたしの いえの ちかく = near my house
- ～で はたらいています = works at...

### 5. Self-introduction patterns
- はじめまして = Nice to meet you
- ～です = I am...
- じこしょうかいを してください = Please introduce yourself
- あなたの なまえは どんな いみですか = What does your name mean?

### 6. Describing activities on days off
- やすみのひに ～をします = on days off, I...
- よく ～に いきます = often go to...
- ～を みる = to look at/see
- ～を かう = to buy
