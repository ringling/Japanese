#### **〜ないで** (nai de)

**Basic info**
• Romaji: nai de
• Hiragana: ないで
• Kanji: 〜ないで
• Word type: Grammar pattern (negative request / "without doing")

**Formation**
1. Take the verb in its plain negative form (〜ない).
2. Add で.
   - Example: 行く (iku = to go) → 行かない (ikanai) → 行かないで (ikanaide)

**Meanings**
1. Negative request/command: “Don’t do …”
   - Often used with ください for politeness: 〜ないでください
2. Expressing “without doing …” when connecting clauses.

**Examples (request)**
• ドアを開けないでください。  
  *Doa o akenaide kudasai.*  
  “Please don’t open the door.”

• タバコを吸わないで。  
  *Tabako o suwanaide.*  
  “Don’t smoke.”

**Examples (without doing)**
• 朝ごはんを食べないで、学校へ行きました。  
  *Asa gohan o tabenaide, gakkō e ikimashita.*  
  “I went to school without eating breakfast.”

• 何も言わないで出て行きました。  
  *Nani mo iwanaide dete ikimashita.*  
  “He left without saying anything.”

#### **〜すぎ (sugi)**

**Basic info**
• Romaji: ~sugi / ~sugiru
• Hiragana: 〜すぎ / 〜すぎる
• Kanji: 〜過ぎ / 〜過ぎる
• Word type: Auxiliary verb (attached to verbs and adjectives)

**Meaning**
• “Too much / excessively”  
• Indicates doing something excessively or more than is good/normal.

**Formation**
1. Verb (ます-stem) + すぎる
   - 食べます (tabemasu) → 食べすぎる (tabesugiru) = to eat too much
   - 飲みます (nomimasu) → 飲みすぎる (nomisugiru) = to drink too much

2. い-adjective (stem without い) + すぎる
   - 高い (takai = expensive) → 高すぎる (takasugiru) = too expensive
   - 寒い (samui = cold) → 寒すぎる (samusugiru) = too cold

3. な-adjective + すぎる
   - 静か (shizuka = quiet) → 静かすぎる (shizukasugiru) = too quiet
   - 便利 (benri = convenient) → 便利すぎる (benrisugiru) = too convenient

**Conjugation of すぎる**
• Plain present: すぎる (sugiru) = do too much  
• Negative plain: すぎない (suginai) = don’t do too much  
• Past plain: すぎた (sugita) = did too much  
• Past negative plain: すぎなかった (suginakatta) = didn’t do too much  
• Polite present: すぎます (sugimasu) = do too much  
• Polite negative: すぎません (sugimasen) = don’t do too much  
• Polite past: すぎました (sugimashita) = did too much  
• Polite past negative: すぎませんでした (sugimasen deshita) = didn’t do too much  

**Examples**
• ケーキを食べすぎました。  
  *Kēki o tabesugimashita.*  
  “I ate too much cake.”

• この靴は高すぎる。  
  *Kono kutsu wa takasugiru.*  
  “These shoes are too expensive.”

• 部屋が静かすぎて、ちょっと気持ち悪い。  
  *Heya ga shizukasugite, chotto kimochi warui.*  
  “The room is too quiet, it feels a bit creepy.”
