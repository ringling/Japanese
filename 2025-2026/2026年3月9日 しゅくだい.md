## Grammar

* なら
	* N1 なら、N2 が いいです （よ）
	* Used when taking up something another person has said and offering a suggestion.
* ので
	* S1（Plain form） ので、S2
	* Used when saying the reason for something.
* の   
	* V ／ イA ／ ナA（Plain form）の は、N です
	* Used when strongly expressing what one wants to say.
* ずつ
	* N（Number）ずつ
	* Used when ordering the same amounts from the menu.
* These are examples of the **〜ずつ** pattern, which means "each" or "at a time."

## **〜ずつ** Pattern:

**Number + Counter + ずつ** = "X each" or "X at a time"

## Breaking down your examples:

### **1つずつ** (hitotsu zutsu)

- **1つ** (hitotsu) - "one (thing)"
- **ずつ** (zutsu) - "each"
- **Translation:** "one each" or "one at a time"

### **2本ずつ** (ni-hon zutsu)

- **2本** (ni-hon) - "two (long cylindrical objects)"
- **ずつ** (zutsu) - "each"
- **Translation:** "two each" or "two at a time"

### **3人ずつ** (san-nin zutsu)

- **3人** (san-nin) - "three people"
- **ずつ** (zutsu) - "each"
- **Translation:** "three people each" or "in groups of three"

## Usage examples:

- **りんごを1つずつ食べました** (ringo wo hitotsu zutsu tabemashita) - "I ate the apples one by one"
- **ペンを2本ずつあげます** (pen wo ni-hon zutsu agemasu) - "I'll give two pens to each person"
- **3人ずつのグループを作ってください** (san-nin zutsu no guruupu wo tsukutte kudasai) - "Please make groups of three people each"

This pattern is very useful for describing distribution, division, or doing things in equal portions!

### ❶ 冷たい 飲み物を 注文しましょう。生の 野菜も 食べたいです。

> **"Let's order a cold drink. I also want to eat raw vegetables."**

|Japanese|Reading|Meaning|
|---|---|---|
|冷たい|つめたい|cold|
|飲み物|のみもの|drink/beverage|
|注文しましょう|ちゅうもんしましょう|let's order|
|生の|なまの|raw|
|野菜|やさい|vegetables|
|食べたいです|たべたいです|want to eat|

---

### ❷ よかったら、この 地方の 料理を 食べてみて ください。

> **"If you'd like, please try eating this region's cuisine."**

|Japanese|Reading|Meaning|
|---|---|---|
|よかったら|—|if you'd like / if it's okay|
|地方の|ちほうの|of the region / local|
|料理|りょうり|cuisine / dish|
|食べてみて|たべてみて|try eating (てみる = try doing)|

---

### ❸ あの 店は 有名な 和食の レストランで、いつも 客が 多いです。

> **"That restaurant is a famous Japanese-food restaurant, and it's always crowded with customers."**

|Japanese|Reading|Meaning|
|---|---|---|
|あの店|あのみせ|that shop/restaurant|
|有名な|ゆうめいな|famous|
|和食|わしょく|Japanese cuisine|
|いつも|—|always|
|客が多い|きゃくがおおい|many customers|

---

### ❹ 兄は 洋食が 好きです。とくに 牛肉や とり肉の 料理を よく 食べます。

> **"My older brother likes Western food. He especially often eats dishes with beef and chicken."**

|Japanese|Reading|Meaning|
|---|---|---|
|兄|あに|older brother|
|洋食|ようしょく|Western-style food|
|とくに|—|especially / particularly|
|牛肉|ぎゅうにく|beef|
|とり肉|とりにく|chicken|
|よく食べます|—|often eats|

---

## s40 2

## Grammar Point 2: 〜ので (because / since)

**S1 (plain form) のので、S2** — S1 is the _reason_ for S2

> この店は おいしいので、よく 来ます。 _"Because this restaurant is delicious, I come often."_

---

### ⚠️ Key Rule: N / な-Adjective + **な**ので

|Type|Example|
|---|---|
|い-adjective|さむい**ので**|
|Verb|うんてんする**ので**|
|な-adjective|有名**なので**|
|Noun|ベジタリアン**なので**|

### 💡 ので vs から

Both mean "because" — のので sounds **softer and more polite**, から is more direct. のので is preferred in formal or considerate speech.
