| -           | Japanese                | English                                       |

| 🍲 Host     | はい、できました。どうぞ。           | Right, it's ready. Please go ahead!           |
| 🙋 Guest    | あのう、どうやって 食べますか。        | Umm, how do you eat it?                       |
| 💭 _thinks_ | そのまま                    | _As it is? / Just like that?_                 |
| 💭 _thinks_ | 何も つけないで                | _Without dipping it in anything?_             |
| 🍲 Host     | たれを つけて 食べて ください。       | Please eat it by dipping it in sauce.         |
| 🙋 Guest    | いただきます。たれを つけて 食べるんですね。 | Let's eat! So you dip it in sauce and eat it. |
| 🍲 Host     | はい。つけすぎる と、からいですよ。      | Yes. If you put on too much, it'll be spicy.  |
| 🙋 Guest    | おいしいですね。                | It's delicious, isn't it!                     |
| 🙋 Guest    | はじめての 味です。              | It's a flavour I've never had before!         |

---

A few highlights worth noting:

**いただきます** — the ritual phrase said before eating in Japan, roughly "I humbly receive." Untranslatable but essential.

**食べるんですね** — the guest uses んです to confirm their understanding ("so _that's_ how you do it, right?"). It shows they've just learned something and are checking they got it right.

**はじめての 味です** — literally "it's a first-time taste." A very natural and charming way to say something tastes completely new to you. はじめて means "for the first time."

## Grammar Spotlight: ～ながら

This pattern means **"while doing ~"** — two actions happening simultaneously.

|Verb (ます-stem)|+ ながら|Meaning|
|---|---|---|
|作り|ながら|while making|
|食べ|ながら|while eating|
|聞き|ながら|while listening|

**Formation:** Drop ます from the verb → add ながら

The **main action** comes at the end of the sentence; the ながら action is secondary/background.

> 音楽を**聞きながら**勉強します。  
> I study **while listening** to music.
