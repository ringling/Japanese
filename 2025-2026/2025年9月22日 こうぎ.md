**Basic info**
• Romaji: yame  
• Hiragana: やめ  
• Kanji: (止め / 辞め) ← context dependent  
• Word type: Verb stem (from やめる)

**Meaning**
• To stop / to quit / to give up  
• やめ is the stem form of やめる (yameru)

**Forms (base verb やめる = to stop, to quit)**
• Dictionary form: やめる (yameru) = to stop / to quit  
• Negative plain: やめない (yamenai) = not stop  
• Past plain: やめた (yameta) = stopped / quit  
• Past negative plain: やめなかった (yamenakatta) = did not stop  
• Polite present: やめます (yamemasu) = stop / quit  
• Polite past: やめました (yamemashita) = stopped / quit  
• Te-form: やめて (yamete) = stop (please stop)  

**Examples**
• タバコをやめました。  
  *Tabako o yamemashita.*  
  “I quit smoking.”

• やめてください。  
  *Yamete kudasai.*  
  “Please stop.”

• その話はやめよう。  
  *Sono hanashi wa yameyō.*  
  “Let’s stop that topic.”

**Basic info**
• Romaji: mago  
• Hiragana: まご  
• Kanji: 孫  
• Word type: Noun

**Meaning**
• Grandchild (can mean grandson or granddaughter)  
• Sometimes also used in a general sense for “descendant”

**Examples**
• 孫が二人います。  
  *Mago ga futari imasu.*  
  “I have two grandchildren.”

• 彼女はかわいい孫の写真を見せてくれました。  
  *Kanojo wa kawaii mago no shashin o misete kuremashita.*  
  “She showed me a picture of her cute grandchild.”

• この家は孫の世代まで残るでしょう。  
  *Kono ie wa mago no sedai made nokoru deshō.*  
  “This house will remain until the grandchildren’s generation.”

**Basic info**
• Romaji: araimasu  
• Hiragana: あらいます  
• Kanji: 洗います  
• Word type: Verb (polite present form)  
• Base verb: 洗う (arau)  
• Verb group: Godan (u-verb)

**Meaning**
• To wash (hands, dishes, clothes, etc.)

**Forms (base verb 洗う = to wash)**
• Dictionary form: 洗う (arau) = to wash  
• Negative plain: 洗わない (arawanai) = don’t wash  
• Past plain: 洗った (aratta) = washed  
• Past negative plain: 洗わなかった (arawanakatta) = didn’t wash  
• Polite present: 洗います (araimasu) = wash / will wash  
• Polite past: 洗いました (araimashita) = washed  
• Te-form: 洗って (aratte) = wash and … / please wash  

**Examples**
• 手を洗います。  
  *Te o araimasu.*  
  “I wash my hands.”

• 毎日、服を洗います。  
  *Mainichi, fuku o araimasu.*  
  “I wash clothes every day.”

• ご飯の前に手を洗ってください。  
  *Gohan no mae ni te o aratte kudasai.*  
  “Please wash your hands before meals.”

**Basic info**
• Romaji: tsukare ga toremasu  
• Hiragana: つかれ が とれます  
• Kanji: 疲れが取れます  
• Word type: Phrase (noun + verb)

**Meaning**
• To recover from tiredness / fatigue goes away  
• Lit. “The tiredness comes off / is removed”

**Grammar breakdown**
• 疲れ (つかれ / tsukare) = tiredness, fatigue (noun from verb 疲れる = to get tired)  
• 取れる (とれる / toreru) = to come off, to be removed, to go away  
• 取れます (toremasu) = polite present form  

**Examples**
• よく寝ると、疲れが取れます。  
  *Yoku neru to, tsukare ga toremasu.*  
  “If you sleep well, your fatigue goes away.”

• お風呂に入ると、疲れが取れますよ。  
  *Ofuro ni hairu to, tsukare ga toremasu yo.*  
  “Taking a bath relieves fatigue.”

• 休みの日に運動すると、疲れが取れます。  
  *Yasumi no hi ni undō suru to, tsukare ga toremasu.*  
  “Exercising on days off helps get rid of fatigue.”

**Basic info**
• Romaji: nome to ii desu yo  
• Hiragana: のめと いいですよ  
• Kanji: 飲めといいですよ  
• Word type: Phrase (imperative + と + いいですよ construction)

**Meaning**
• “It would be good if you drink (it).”  
• Giving advice or recommendation to drink (e.g., water, medicine).  

**Grammar breakdown**
1. 飲め (nome) = imperative form of 飲む (nomu = to drink)  
2. と = quotation particle (“that …”)  
3. いいですよ (ii desu yo) = it’s good / it would be good (soft, friendly advice)  

So together: **飲めといいですよ = It’s good if you drink (it).**

**Examples**
• 薬を飲めといいですよ。  
  *Kusuri o nome to ii desu yo.*  
  “It would be good if you take the medicine.”

• 水を飲めといいですよ。  
  *Mizu o nome to ii desu yo.*  
  “It would be good if you drink water.”
